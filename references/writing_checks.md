# Writing Checks

Use this file when the PDF reads correctly visually but still sounds generic, overly polished, or context-dependent.

## Core standard

Good report prose should feel:

- specific
- self-contained
- honest about tradeoffs
- written by a person who actually made the decisions

## Rewrite checklist

- Replace insider shorthand with plain language unless the term is explained immediately.
- Do not assume the reader saw earlier drafts, internal notes, or the build process.
- Say what was decided, why it was decided, and what tradeoff it implied.
- Cut repeated ideas across sections. Keep the point in the strongest location.
- Prefer one concrete sentence over two vague sentences.
- If a section sounds like a polished placeholder, rewrite it with concrete nouns and decisions.

## Common anti-slop rewrites

Instead of:

- "This robust framework enables..."
- "The final artifact reflects..."
- "The solution balances..."
- "The approach leverages..."

Prefer:

- what was actually done
- what constraint mattered
- what changed after review
- why one option was chosen over another

## Addendum rule

For short addenda, default to a small number of clear sections. Do not make a one-page memo sound like a board deck.

## Human-verification note

When writing about AI use:

- be precise about what context was shared with AI and what was not
- separate AI assistance from human judgment
- name at least one improvement or decision that clearly required human expertise
